About Me

I'm a data scientist and research scientist with a Ph.D. in Physics from the University of Houston, where I discovered that my real passion is using quantitative methods to understand complex human biology. Since graduating in 2023, I've worked at the intersection of machine learning, biostatistics, and translational research.

At the GENES:IS Lab (TIMES, UH), I serve as a data science and ML methodologist for an interdisciplinary team of psychologists, geneticists, linguists, and statisticians studying cognitive and developmental outcomes in adolescents. I've built end-to-end pipelines integrating MRI neuroimaging, genetic data, EEG, microbiome assays, and behavioral assessments.

In parallel, at INAU.ai, I design data collection and ML architectures for wearable biosensor research and have identified over $2.7M in funding for novel AI applications in autism spectrum disorder research.

I thrive in collaborative, cross-disciplinary settings where I can bridge the gap between domain expertise and rigorous quantitative analysis — whether advising on study design, engineering features from raw biological signals, or communicating results to non-technical stakeholders.

Neuroimaging Genomics ML Pipeline

Brain-Imaging Genetic Analysis of Academic Skills

Which genetic markers shape brain development in ways that predict academic achievement?

Built a three-stage pipeline linking individual genetic variants to academic outcomes (reading, math, comprehension) via structural MRI intermediaries — gray matter volume and white matter integrity — in ~1,600 participants. Handled cross-modal imputation, imaging feature engineering, and mediation analysis across three collaborating disciplines.

N ≈ 1,600 MRI + SNPs + Behavior Mediation Analysis Python / R
Neuroimaging Multimodal ML

Multimodal Prediction of Cognitive Ability (WISC-V)

Can structural brain features reliably predict IQ across a large, diverse pediatric cohort?

Developed a multimodal ML pipeline predicting Full-Scale IQ from PCA-compressed gray matter and white matter features (10 + 10 components) in N = 782 children from the Child Mind Institute HBN dataset. Three nested model conditions isolated the relative contribution of each imaging modality.

N = 782 WISC-V / FSIQ PCA + Nested CV CMI HBN
EEG Classification

Detecting Generational Effects of Institutionalization via EEG

Can brain electrophysiology reveal the hidden developmental toll of early childhood deprivation?

Designed and benchmarked a comparative classification battery on EEG data to distinguish children with institutional care histories from matched controls. Permutation testing provided empirically derived p-values, ensuring statistically rigorous evaluation beyond standard cross-validation.

EEG Classifier Battery Permutation Testing
NLP Classification

Predicting Learning Disorders from Written Text

What can a child's writing reveal about underlying reading and language disorders?

Extracted linguistic features from children's written samples and applied mutual information-based feature selection to identify markers most predictive of diagnosed learning disorders. Benchmarked a battery of classifiers to assess generalizability across disorder subtypes.

Text Feature Engineering Mutual Information FS Multi-class Classification
Pharmacokinetics ODE Modeling

Circadian PK/PD Modeling for Gemcitabine in Pancreatic Cancer

Does the time of chemotherapy administration matter — and can we model why?

Built an ODE-based pharmacokinetic / pharmacodynamic model incorporating circadian gene expression rhythms (DCK, CDA, NT5C2) to simulate how biological time-of-day alters gemcitabine efficacy across 10 pancreatic cancer cell lines. Reduced model structure identified the dominant regulatory axes.

ODE Systems 10 Cell Lines Circadian Biology PDAC
Interpretable ML Inflammation

Adiposity, Inflammation & Cytokine Dynamics

How does excess adiposity dysregulate the immune system, and which cytokines drive the signal?

Modeled 70 Olink protein biomarkers against adiposity and leukocyte composition measures in two independent cohorts (CCHC and UTHealth). Applied quantile regression to characterize distributional effects and SHAP values to rank cytokine importance across adiposity strata.

70 Olink Cytokines Quantile Regression SHAP Two Cohorts

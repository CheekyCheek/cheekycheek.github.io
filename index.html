About Me

My path from theoretical physics to biomedical research wasn't a detour — it's the reason I bring a rigorous modeling mindset to problems that most biologists find intractable and most computer scientists find opaque.

I thrive at the boundary between disciplines, where I can bridge domain expertise and rigorous quantitative analysis — whether designing a study, engineering features from raw biological signals, or translating results for non-technical stakeholders.

Neuroimaging Genomics ML Pipeline

Brain-Imaging Genetic Analysis of Academic Skills

Which genetic markers shape brain development in ways that predict academic achievement?

Built a three-stage pipeline linking genetic variants to academic outcomes (reading, math, comprehension) via structural MRI intermediaries. A data-driven feature selection step reduced the genomic feature space from ~180,000 to ~4,000 SNPs. A simulation study (n = 2,000, p = 50–500k) benchmarked classifier performance. End result: 24 coding genes associated with reading ability and reading-related gray matter structure identified.

180k → 4k SNPs 24 Coding Genes MRI + Genomics Simulation Study
Neuroimaging Multimodal ML

Multimodal Prediction of Cognitive Ability (WISC-V)

Can structural brain features reliably predict IQ across a large, diverse pediatric cohort?

Developed a multimodal ML pipeline predicting Full-Scale IQ from PCA-compressed gray matter and white matter features (10 + 10 components) in N = 782 children from the Child Mind Institute HBN dataset. Three nested model conditions isolated the relative contribution of each imaging modality.

N = 782 WISC-V / FSIQ PCA + Nested CV CMI HBN
EEG Classification

Detecting Generational Effects of Institutionalization via EEG

Can brain electrophysiology reveal the hidden developmental toll of early childhood deprivation?

Developed ML pipelines for small-N EEG datasets using pooled neural responses to a language task. The core challenge: standard CV gives unreliable estimates at small sample sizes. Applied empirical permutation-based significance testing to demonstrate robust model performance (p < 0.05) despite limited data — a methodological contribution as much as an applied one.

Small-N Methods EEG / Language Task Permutation p < 0.05 Classifier Battery
NLP Classification

Predicting Learning Disorders from Written Text

What can a child's writing reveal about underlying reading and language disorders?

Designed ML pipelines to predict learning disability status (p < 0.05) on heavily imbalanced datasets (25:75 ratio). Applied mutual information-based feature selection for experimental NLP indices. Extended to scanned handwriting samples using MobileNetV2 transfer learning, achieving AUC ~70% — demonstrating multi-modal signal across both text and image modalities.

AUC ~70% (MobileNetV2) Imbalanced Learning Mutual Information FS Transfer Learning
Pharmacokinetics ODE Modeling

Circadian PK/PD Modeling for Gemcitabine in Pancreatic Cancer

Does the time of chemotherapy administration matter — and can we model why?

Built an ODE-based pharmacokinetic / pharmacodynamic model incorporating circadian gene expression rhythms (DCK, CDA, NT5C2) to simulate how biological time-of-day alters gemcitabine efficacy across 10 pancreatic cancer cell lines. Reduced model structure identified the dominant regulatory axes.

ODE Systems 10 Cell Lines Circadian Biology PDAC
Interpretable ML Inflammation

Adiposity, Inflammation & Cytokine Dynamics

How does excess adiposity dysregulate the immune system, and which cytokines drive the signal?

Modeled 70 Olink protein biomarkers against adiposity and leukocyte composition measures in two independent cohorts (CCHC and UTHealth). Applied quantile regression to characterize distributional effects and SHAP values to rank cytokine importance across adiposity strata.

70 Olink Cytokines Quantile Regression SHAP Two Cohorts
